Modelling Recyclable Materials Collected and Recycled of Victoria

The goal of the project is to help stakeholders understand the current state of waste management in Victoria, evaluate historical performance of recycling facilities, and help them in understanding the impact of reformed policies on future waste recycling based on the forecasts obtained from machine learning models. Forecasted values include the number of kerbside recyclable materials collected and recycled by the local councils in Victoria until 2039. Incorporating demographics of the Local Government Area for forecasting is another important aspect of the project. Knowing how much waste will be collected in the future will ensure that Victoria's recycling facilities can handle the volume of recyclable materials collected. This will help in reducing the amount of waste exported to other countries or sent to landfill or storage. Using forecasted figures, the feasibility of policy reforms can be grasped, and community outreach projects can be implemented by policy makers to minimise waste in the early stages. Knowing how much material is not recycled will make it easier to make policy changes to reduce the number of materials not recycled. This will also help in raising public awareness on the importance of waste management thus encouraging people to be more enthusiastic about recycling and lead to further improvement in Victoria’s waste management capabilities.

The project displays the results and findings after data pre-processing and modelling steps using a dashboard which visualizes the past, and the predicted values for waste collected and recycled for different councils in Victoria. Since the intended stakeholders include policy makers of Victoria, the dashboard is interactive so that they can see predicted values as well as experiment with the impacts of different policies they may want to implement in future to improve Victoria’s waste collection and recycling capabilities.
